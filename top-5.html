My Top 5 Lego Sets

Home About Me Top 5

Number 5:

Lego Ferrari F8 Tributo

I recently started collecting the 8 stud Speed Champions line sports cars, and this is one of the first ones made. The sleek design and color really makes it stand out from the others.

Number 4:

Deep Sea Striker

I have a weird liking for giant animals made deadly, and this set is just perfect when it comes to that liking. I was honestly considering putting it higher on the list, but a scorpion placing in first would be kind of weird. The color scheme looks amazing too!

Number 3:

Medieval Blacksmith

This set is pretty good, was a good value before lego increased the price, and the black falcons starred here are awesome because that is my favorite LEGO medieval faction.

Number 2:

Witch Doctor

Giant Hero Factory villain, is poseable, and looks super cool. I got this from a trade this with my brother for some DS games a while back. I don't regret it.

Honorable Mention:

Stingray Stormer

I got this from a vintage toy shop in Sandusky for a really good price. it is from 1998, and while I no longer own the set, it still holds place in my memory as a relic in LEGO's history.

Number 1:

Vladek's Dark Fortress

My friend recently sold me this one, and it was not only a great build, but it looks amazing to display. The old castle sets from the 2000s have a special charm that the new ones just can't match.
